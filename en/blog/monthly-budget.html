How to Build a Monthly Budget That Actually Works

5 min read · By the WorthOne team

A budget isn't a cage – it's a plan that gives your money direction. Most budgets fail because they're too complicated or too rigid. Here's a simple approach that's easy to stick to.

Step 1: Find your take-home income

Start with your monthly net income – the amount that actually lands in your account after taxes. That's the base you build everything on.

Step 2: Split with the 50/30/20 method

A simple, proven way to divide your income:

  • 50% needs: rent/mortgage, food, bills, transport.
  • 30% wants: outings, shopping, subscriptions, vacations.
  • 20% future: saving, investing and paying down debt.

The ratios aren't sacred – adapt them to your reality, but keep a fixed slice for "future."

Step 3: Track for a month

Record every expense for a month (a banking app or a simple spreadsheet). The goal isn't to judge yourself but to understand your real patterns – that's where the surprises show up.

Step 4: Adjust and keep going

A budget is a living document. Review it at the end of each month, adjust categories that overran, and celebrate small wins. Consistency matters more than perfection – an imperfect budget you stick to beats a perfect one you abandon.
