How to Get Out of Debt: Practical Strategies

6 min read · By the WorthOne team

Debt can feel like a bottomless pit, but with an organized plan you can climb out – step by step. The key is to stop the slide, and then attack the debt systematically.

First step: stop and map

Before anything, list all your debts in one place: the amount, the interest rate and the minimum monthly payment of each. Without a full picture you can't plan. At the same time, avoid adding new debt – that's the first and most important step.

Method 1: the snowball

Order your debts from smallest to largest, and attack the smallest first (while paying the minimum on the rest). Once it's cleared, roll that money into the next debt. The advantage: quick wins that build motivation and a sense of control.

Method 2: the avalanche

Order your debts by interest rate, from highest to lowest, and attack the most expensive first. Mathematically this saves the most money over time, because it "puts out the biggest fire" first.

Which method to choose

If you need a psychological boost – the snowball. If you're numbers-focused and want to save the most – the avalanche. Both work; the best one is the one you'll actually stick to.

A key tip

Check whether you can lower your interest rates – consolidating loans or moving to a cheaper track can significantly speed up your exit from debt. Every percentage point of interest saved is money that comes back to you.
