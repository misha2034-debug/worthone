Compound Interest: The Force That Grows Your Money

4 min read · By the WorthOne team

If there's one concept every saver needs to understand, it's compound interest. It's the difference between savings that crawl and savings that leap – and its secret is remarkably simple.

What it actually is

"Simple" interest is calculated only on the original amount. Compound interest is calculated also on the gains you've already earned. In other words: your gains start generating gains of their own. As time passes, this effect accelerates and creates a curve that keeps climbing.

Why time matters more than the amount

The real power of compounding reveals itself over decades. Someone who starts investing a modest sum at 25 may end up with more than someone who starts with a larger amount at 40 – simply because the money had more years to "roll." That's why the best advice in finance is: start early, even if small.

An illustrative example

Suppose an average annual return of about 7%. Depositing ₪500 a month for 30 years amounts to ₪180,000 that you deposited – but thanks to compounding, the final sum is far larger. Most of the growth comes in the final years, when the "snowball" is biggest.

The practical takeaway

Don't wait for the "perfect" moment. Time is the most precious resource in saving, and the only one you can't get back. An early, consistent start is worth more than any attempt to time the market.
