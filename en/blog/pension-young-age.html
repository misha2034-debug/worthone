Pension: Why You Should Care While You're Young

5 min read · By the WorthOne team

At 25, retirement sounds like science fiction, so it's easy to ignore your pension. But precisely when you're young, with the most time until retirement, small decisions turn into enormous differences.

How a pension actually works

Each month a portion of your salary is set aside into a pension fund – part from you and part from your employer. The money is invested over the years, and when you retire it funds a monthly annuity. The more that accumulates and the longer it grows, the higher the annuity.

Why being young is a huge advantage

Thanks to compound interest, the money deposited when you're young are the hardest-working "employees" – they have decades to grow. A few years of delay at the start can cost large amounts in your future annuity.

What to check right now

  • Management fees: small percentages compound into an enormous difference over a career. It's worth comparing and negotiating.
  • Investment track: when young, you can often choose a track with higher stock exposure, according to the risk level that suits you.
  • Insurance coverage: make sure the coverage in the fund fits your situation and isn't excessive.

The first step

Simply log into your pension report once a year and understand where you stand. The mere awareness – how much has accumulated, what the fees are, which track – already puts you a step ahead of most people.
