5 Common Money Management Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

5 min read · By the WorthOne team

Managing money doesn't require genius – just avoiding a few common traps. Here are five mistakes that recur for many people, and what to do instead.

1. Not knowing where the money goes

It's hard to manage what you don't measure. Many people are surprised when they discover how much goes out each month on small items. A simple month or two of tracking reveals patterns and lets you make informed decisions.

2. No emergency fund

Without a safety cushion, every surprise – a car repair, a spell of unemployment – turns into high-interest debt. An emergency fund of three to six months of expenses is the foundation that prevents a financial fall.

3. Ignoring management fees

One percent of fees sounds trivial, but over decades it eats a significant chunk of your savings thanks to compounding. Checking and comparing the fees on your funds is one of the most worthwhile moves you can make.

4. Holding expensive debt alongside savings

There's no logic in saving at a few percent while paying double-digit interest on credit or loans. Paying off expensive debt is usually the "investment" with the highest guaranteed return.

5. Not seeing the whole picture

When money is scattered across checking, funds, pension and investments, it's hard to understand the real situation. Bringing all assets into one place lets you see your net worth, spot imbalances and act.
