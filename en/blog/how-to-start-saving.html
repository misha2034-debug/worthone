How to Start Saving: A Practical Beginner's Guide

5 min read · By the WorthOne team

Most of us know we should save, but there's a big gap between intention and action. The good news: saving is a habit, not an inborn talent. With a few simple rules you can make it an automatic part of life – without feeling like you're giving up everything.

1. Pay yourself first

The most important principle in saving: don't wait to see "what's left" at the end of the month – because usually nothing is. Instead, the moment your salary arrives, move a fixed amount to savings before you spend on anything else. Even 5% is a great start.

2. Set a clear, concrete goal

"Saving" is vague. "Saving ₪15,000 for an emergency fund by year end" is a goal. A measurable target with a deadline creates motivation and makes every deposit meaningful. Break a big goal into monthly sub-goals to see progress.

3. Make it automatic

The single biggest boost to success is a standing order. Set up an automatic transfer on payday from your checking account to a separate savings account. What you don't see is less tempting to spend. Automation beats willpower every time.

4. Cut where it truly matters

Instead of agonizing over every coffee, look at the big, fixed expenses: subscriptions you don't use, interest on credit, phone plans or insurance you can lower. Focusing on a few big items yields more than dozens of tiny sacrifices.

5. Track your progress

What gets measured improves. Seeing your savings grow builds momentum to keep going. Reviewing the totals across all your accounts periodically makes saving tangible and encourages you to stick with it.
