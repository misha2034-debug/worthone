Your First ₪100k in Your 20s: The Seed of Financial Freedom

8 min read · By the WorthOne team

If you ask older people what they'd do differently at 25, you'll hear one answer again and again: "I'd have started saving earlier." The first ₪100,000 you accumulate in your 20s is perhaps the most important money you'll ever save — not because of the amount itself, but because of what it represents and what it will become. This article shows why this milestone matters so much, how to reach it even on an entry-level salary, and how it's the seed of future financial independence.

Why the first ₪100k specifically

There are two reasons, and they reinforce each other. The first is mathematical: money saved at 25 gets the greatest gift of time — about 40 years of compounding until retirement. Thanks to exponential growth, ₪100k accumulated at 25 may grow many times over by retirement, without you adding another shekel. The same ₪100k invested at 40 simply won't get those powerful years.

The second reason is behavioral: the first hundred thousand is proof to yourself that you can do it. It builds the habit, the muscle, and the confidence that stay with you for life. Once you've seen the number climb from zero to six figures, the rest of the journey no longer looks impossible — it looks like more of the same.

The math is more encouraging than it seems

It looks like an enormous sum, but let's break it down (assuming an average annual return of 7%, for illustration only): a deposit of about ₪1,200 a month reaches ₪100k in roughly 6 years; a deposit of about ₪2,000 a month shortens that to about 4 years. In other words — someone who starts at 24–25 and deposits an amount that's possible even on an entry-level salary can absolutely cross the ₪100k line before 30. And that's before counting a pension fund and study fund, which run in parallel in the background.

The practical formula: widen the gap

The amount left to save is simply the difference between what comes in and what goes out. So there are only two levers, and both are in your hands:

  • Rein in the big expenses. Not the coffee — the rent, the car, the subscriptions. Early on, flatmates or temporarily skipping a car can free up thousands a month. Those are the decisions that truly matter.
  • Grow your income. Precisely at the start of your career, investing in yourself — an in-demand skill, a promotion, a job change — can boost your salary by tens of percent. And any raise that isn't "swallowed" by a higher lifestyle becomes savings directly.

The rule that ties them together: pay yourself first. A standing order on payday that moves a fixed amount to savings before you've started spending — so accumulation happens automatically, and expenses adjust to what's left.

Use the country's "tailwind"

In your 20s, you have engines running in parallel to your personal saving, and it would be a shame to miss them: a pension fund that both you and your employer deposit into, and a study fund where, for every shekel you deposit, the employer usually deposits three, with a tax exemption on the profit. These are effectively "free money" and tax benefits that accelerate wealth-building with no extra effort. Make sure they're active, that fees are reasonable, and that the investment track suits a young age.

How this connects to financial independence

Here the loop closes. Financial independence — the point where your capital covers your living expenses — is measured by your "freedom number" (roughly annual spending times 25). That number sounds astronomical and distant when you start from zero. But the first ₪100k is the first rung of that ladder, and it's the hardest rung to climb — because it's almost entirely from deposits, with no help from returns.

Once you cross it, something happens: compounding starts to participate. The second milestone arrives faster than the first, the third faster than the second, and so on — until one day the annual return on your portfolio exceeds what you deposit, and the money starts working harder than you do. The ₪100k you save at 27 isn't just savings; it's the seed from which, over decades, the freedom to choose grows.

The bottom line

Don't underestimate the power of the first milestone. It's the hardest, the slowest, and the most important. Reach the first ₪100k in your 20s — with a combination of automatic saving, reining in the big expenses, using pension and study-fund benefits, and growing your income — and you'll find you've laid the foundation for everything else. Your 50-year-old self will thank you.
