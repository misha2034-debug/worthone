Emergency Fund: How Much You Need and How to Build It

4 min read · By the WorthOne team

An emergency fund is perhaps the most important financial step before any other saving or investing. It's a safety cushion that separates an "annoying surprise" from a "financial crisis."

Why you need one

Life is full of surprises: a car breakdown, medical treatment, a layoff. Without liquid money set aside, each such event pushes you toward high-interest loans or selling investments at a bad time. An emergency fund buys you peace of mind and time.

How much you need

A common rule of thumb: three to six months of expenses. If your income is very stable, three months may be enough; if you're self-employed or have variable income, aim for six or more. Importantly, calculate it based on your monthly expenses, not your income.

Where to hold it

The key word is liquidity. The money should be available within a day or two, not locked up and not exposed to market swings. A liquid deposit or a money market fund are common places – they keep value relatively stable and allow quick access.

How to build it

Don't wait until you have a large sum all at once. Set up a small automatic deposit each month, and treat it like a fixed bill. Even ₪300–500 a month adds up to a meaningful cushion within a year or two.
