Getting Started in the Stock Market: The Basics

7 min read · By the WorthOne team

Investing sounds intimidating to the uninitiated, but the basic principles are simple. The goal isn't to "time the market" but to let your money work for you over time.

The concepts worth knowing

  • Stock: a small piece of ownership in a company. Its value rises and falls with the company's success.
  • Bond: a loan you give to a government or company in exchange for interest. Usually less volatile than stocks.
  • Index fund: a fund that tracks an entire index (for example, the 500 largest US companies), offering broad diversification at low cost.

Three guiding principles

Diversify: don't put all your eggs in one basket. An index fund gives exposure to hundreds of companies at once.

Time: the longer you invest, the less individual swings matter and the more power compounding has.

Costs: low management fees leave more money in your pocket over time. The difference adds up to large amounts.

First steps before investing

  1. Pay off expensive debt – that's a guaranteed "return."
  2. Build an emergency fund so you're not forced to sell at a loss at a bad moment.
  3. Define a time horizon and goal for each investment.
  4. Start with small, regular amounts to learn as you go.

The most common mistake

Trying to guess when to get in and out. Many studies show that staying invested over time beats attempts at timing. Consistency and discipline are worth more than a flash of luck.
