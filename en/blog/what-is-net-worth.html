What Is Net Worth and How to Calculate It

4 min read · By the WorthOne team

If you had to pick one number that sums up your financial situation, it would be your net worth. It matters more than your salary, because it measures not how much you earn, but how much you actually keep.

The simple definition

Net worth is a simple formula: everything you own minus everything you owe. Your assets are all the money and property you hold; your liabilities are all your debts. The difference between them is your net worth.

What counts as assets

  • Balances in checking accounts and deposits
  • Provident funds, study funds and pension funds
  • Investment portfolios and mutual funds
  • Real estate you own (at market value)
  • Other assets of significant value

What counts as liabilities

  • Outstanding mortgage balance
  • Loans (car, personal, student)
  • Credit card debt

Why you should track it

A single number doesn't tell the whole story – but the trend does. A net worth that grows year over year is the clearest sign you're on the right track, even if your income stays flat. Measuring periodically turns abstract goals into a concrete number you can improve.
